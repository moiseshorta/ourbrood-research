## Summary

This critical paper examines power dynamics, sovereignty, and agency negotiation in human-AI collaborative improvisational music performance. Using a decolonial lens, it questions who holds power in human-AI creative partnerships and explores how AI can be positioned not as a tool or assistant but as an entangled participant in co-creative processes.

## Key Theoretical Frameworks

### Sovereignty vs. Entanglement

**Sovereignty:**
- Human maintains control
- AI as instrument/tool
- Clear boundaries of agency
- Traditional HCI model

**Entanglement:**
- Human and AI as unified system
- Agency emerges from interaction
- Mutual influence
- Challenges tool/assistant paradigm

### Decolonial Perspective

- Questions default power structures in technology
- Examines whose interests AI serves
- Challenges Western assumptions about creativity
- Opens space for non-dominant creative practices
