- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.XXXXX (verify)

- **Key Questions:**

  - What mechanisms manage dramatic tension in collective experiences?

## Thematic Analysis

### Key Themes for OurBrood

#### 1. Agency Distribution
Multiple papers explore how agency is distributed between AI systems and human participants. The spectrum ranges from:
- **AI as tool** (delegation model)
- **AI as collaborator** (synergistic co-creation)
- **AI as facilitator** (Game Master / Drama Machine model)

RGP methodology suggests the facilitator model is most appropriate - AI supports collective human experience rather than directing it.

#### 2. Embodied vs. Screen-Based Participation
Several papers critique text-only interfaces and advocate for:
- Body prompting / gestural interaction
- Sound-based co-creation
- Multi-sensory installation contexts

This aligns with RGP's immersive, embodied methodology (3-hour high-intensity experiences).

#### 3. Narrative Architecture
Papers propose various narrative architectures:
- **Decomposition and linking** (StoryComposerAI)
- **Drama machine coordination** (multi-agent character simulation)
- **Immersive role-playing frameworks** (AdaMARP)

Key question: How structured vs. emergent should narrative be?

#### 4. Evaluation Challenges
CharacterBox and other papers raise questions about:
- How to measure immersion and agency
- What makes AI characters "believable" in participatory contexts
- Balancing engagement with meaningful transformation

#### 5. Power and Sovereignty
The "nomadic improvisational music" paper introduces crucial questions about:
- Who holds power in human-AI co-creative systems
- How agency is negotiated in real-time
- Decolonial perspectives on AI in participatory art

This directly connects to OurBrood's focus on "negotiating responsibility" and "testing how we live together."

## Gaps and Opportunities for OurBrood

### Research Gaps

1. **Longitudinal participatory experiences**
   - Most papers focus on single-session interactions
   - RGP's multi-session/high-intensity format is underexplored

2. **Collective improvisation**
   - Many papers focus on individual-AI interaction
   - Group dynamics with AI facilitation need more study

3. **Ethics of AI-guided self-exploration**
   - Limited research on psychological safety
   - Transformation vs. entertainment framing

4. **Museum/gallery deployment studies**
   - Technical papers often lack real-world deployment data
   - Audience studies in institutional contexts needed

2. Study collective improvisational dynamics with AI facilitation
3. Develop evaluation frameworks for participatory AI art
